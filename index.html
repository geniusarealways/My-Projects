Push-Up Variations

Standard Push-Up

Form: Hands shoulder-width apart, body straight from head to heels.

Movement: Lower chest toward floor, elbows at ~45Â°, push back up.

Muscles: Chest, shoulders, triceps, core.

Wide Push-Up

Form: Hands wider than shoulders.

Focus: Emphasizes chest muscles.

Tip: Keep elbows slightly higher to avoid shoulder strain.

Close Push-Up (Diamond)

Form: Hands close, thumbs and index fingers form a diamond.

Focus: Triceps and inner chest.

Challenge: More difficult due to reduced leverage.

Incline Push-Up

Form: Hands on elevated surface (e.g., bench or wall).

Use: Beginner-friendly; reduces load.

Progress: Lower surface as strength improves.

Decline Push-Up

Form: Feet elevated, hands on floor.

Focus: Upper chest and shoulders.

Advanced: Requires strong core and shoulder stability.

Knee Push-Up

Form: Knees on ground, body from knees to head straight.

Use: Great for beginners building strength.

Goal: Transition to full push-ups over time.
